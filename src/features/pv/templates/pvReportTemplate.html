1.5 Project Electrical Scheme

{{clientName}} has chosen {{module_make}}'s {{module_type}} technology photovoltaic (PV) modules and {{Centra_inverter_make}}'s power conversion station solution for this project. The PV modules are placed on {{tracker_make}}'s single-axis trackers with a one-portrait (1P) configuration. We have three variations of tracker tables, with each table capable of accommodating {{tracker_module_1}} modules ({{tracker_type_1}} strings), {{tracker_module_2}} modules ({{tracker_type_2}} strings), and {{tracker_module_3}} modules ({{tracker_type_3}} strings).

In a sequential configuration, {{modules_series}} modules with Power rating of {{module_wp1}} and {{module_wp2}} will be linkedin a daisy chain to form a single string. Each tracker table accommodates {{tracker_type_1}} strings, {{tracker_type_2}} strings or {{tracker_type_3}} strings. Every set of 2 strings will be interconnected using the DC harness system of 4 and 2 string trackers, the harness system connected to the Trunk system through Tee drops and 2 strings will be interconnected using DC Harness system and 1 string will be interconnected without harness system of 3 string trackers to the trunk system through Tee drops. Each trunk System is designed to connect a maximum of 20 strings in parallel, and each individual trunk System is linked to its respective Load Break Disconnect (LBD) as the input circuit.

The output circuits, represented by the DC homerun cables, from the LBDs are linked to the PCS through a combination of both direct burial and above-ground cable management system arrangements. Each PCS is connected with maximum of {{max_lbd_per_pcs}} LBDs.

Each PCS is equipped with a Central Inverter, MV transformer and associated switchgear rated at {{Centarl_inverter_rating}} kVA AC capacity. {{Centarl_inverter_rating}}kVA Central Inverter has a provision for connecting 28 numbers of DC homerun circuits. Each homerun circuit is protected with a fuse and fuse rating is selected based on the number of strings connected at each Trunk System which is 400A or 500A. Inverter AC connections are protected with circuit breakers and connected to LV side of Transformer through internal arrangement in PCS.

MV Transformer is rated for {{mv_voltage}}/{{lv_voltage}}, {{mv_transformer}}, KNAN type. MV side of the transformer is protected with Expulsion fuse, Backup fuse and Two Position Load Break Switch for system isolation.

Each PCS is equipped with a feature allowing it to connect or loop with nearby PCS units on the medium-voltage (MV) side of the transformer. A maximum of Seven ({{pcs_loop_count}}) such PCS units can be looped together, forming an integrated system. The final medium-voltage (MV) feeder resulting from this looped configuration is then connected to the PV plant substation. Each feeder circuit is designed to handle {{feeder_capacity}} MW maximum, and in total, there are Twelve ({{total_feeders}}) feeder circuits connected to the substation, contributing to the overall power distribution within the photovoltaic (PV) plant.

The total collected power through {{mv_voltage}}kV MV feeders in the PV plant substation shall be stepped up to {{poi_voltage}}kV voltage level with the help of main power transformers (MPT) and generated power shall be evacuated at the {{delivery_company}} switching station. The scope of PVI has been limited up to the PV Plant Substation.

2.2 String Sizing Calculation & Methodology

The {{plant_name}} power plant shall be designed for a 1500 Vdc system. It is imperative that when we design the string sizing, the maximum voltage should not exceed the rated maximum voltage which is 1500 Vdc under any operating condition. The ambient temperature plays an important role in the voltage and current characteristics of the PV module. As the temperature decreases, the voltage of the PV module increases. We must ensure that even at the lowest temperature, the voltage across the modules connected in series (string) should not exceed the design voltage of 1500 Vdc.

Apart from ambient temperature, the following factors affect string sizing:

Bifacial Factor

The modules currently employed in the {{plant_name}} power project utilize {{module_type}} technology. Bifacial modules are capable of capturing irradiance from the rear side, adding to the overall power generation. This factor is referred to as the Bifacial factor. The Bifacial factor’s magnitude is primarily influenced by the site’s albedo. An increase in the albedo factor shall correspondingly raise the gain in rear side irradiance. Hence, when determining the string sizing, it’s crucial to account for the highest possible Bifacial factor under the lowest ambient temperature prevailing at the site.

The proposed project modules are {{module_make}} {{module_model}} series modules.

2.3 Maximum Circuit Current, Bifacial Gain

Since the modules used in the project are {{module_type}}, a bifacial factor of {{bifacial_factor}} is adopted for estimating the maximum current as per the inputs provided in the technical datasheet of the module manufacturer.

The maximum PV source circuit current shall be calculated in accordance with NEC 690.8(A)(1)(2) using one of the following methods:

  • 690.8(A)(1)(a)(1): The sum of parallel-connected PV module rated short circuit currents multiplied by 125%.
  • 690.8(A)(1)(a)(2): The calculated maximum current value shall be based on the highest 3-hour current average resulting from the simulated local irradiance on the PV array accounting for elevation and orientation.

As outlined in the informational notes of NEC 690.8(A)(1)(a), one of the industry-standard method for determining the maximum current of a PV system is available in the reference SAND 2004- 3535, titled “Photovoltaic Array Performance Model”, developed by Sandia National Laboratories. This model is used by the System Advisor Model (SAM) simulation program, which is offered by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL).

The PV module parameters corresponding to the highest Wp bin range i.e., ({{max_module_power}} Wp) Wp module have been utilized in this analysis. The necessary module parameter inputs have been provided in the SAM. For this simulation, DC losses have been assumed at zero percent, ensuring that the calculated Isc accurately represents the conditions at the module array.

4 Energy Generation Estimation

4.1 Understanding Annual Energy Yield - Methodology

For the proposed PV power plant, annual energy yield is defined as the amount of energy fed into the grid after due consideration of all types of generation and distribution losses. The solar PV-based power plant comprises optical energy input and electrical output.

The optical energy is essentially dependent on the geographical location, seasonal conditions, climatic conditions, and operating parameters with time.

Electrical output is dependent on the technical specifications of electrical equipment utilized. PVsyst, the industry-standard software, which is used to measure energy yields- has been used for the Energy Generation Assessment.

The analysis was conducted using the .PAN file for the highest bin wattage {{620Wp}} module.
